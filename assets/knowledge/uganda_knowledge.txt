=== AGRICULTURE & FARMING ===

SEASONS:
Uganda has two rainy seasons.
First rains (Season A): March to May - plant maize, beans, groundnuts, sorghum, millet.
Second rains (Season B): September to November - plant cassava, sweet potatoes, vegetables, beans.
Dry seasons: December-February and June-August. Good for harvesting, land clearing, drying crops.
Lake Victoria basin gets more rain than northern Uganda.

MAJOR CROPS BY REGION:
Central Uganda (Buganda): Matoke (matooke), coffee, vanilla, passion fruit, maize.
Northern Uganda (Acholi, Lango): Millet, sorghum, sesame, groundnuts, cotton.
Western Uganda (Ankole, Kigezi): Tea, coffee, Irish potatoes, maize, dairy farming.
Eastern Uganda (Busoga, Bugisu): Sugarcane, maize, coffee (Arabica on Mt Elgon), rice.

MATOKE FARMING:
Plant suckers in March or September. Space 3m x 3m apart.
Mulch heavily to keep moisture. Apply potassium-rich fertilizer.
Harvest after 9-12 months. Signs of readiness: leaves yellowing, fingers filling out.
Common pest: banana weevil. Use clean planting materials and remove dead leaves.
Disease: Black Sigatoka - use recommended fungicides, plant resistant varieties.

COFFEE FARMING:
Uganda grows Robusta (mainly central/western) and Arabica (Mt Elgon, Rwenzori).
Plant in partial shade. Prune after harvest season.
UCDA (Uganda Coffee Development Authority) provides free training - call 0414 234 945.
Best prices: sell through cooperatives, not middlemen.
Harvest: October-February for Robusta, October-January for Arabica.

SOIL TYPES:
Red laterite soils - Central Uganda. Good for coffee, matoke, cassava.
Sandy loam - Eastern Uganda. Good for maize, beans, groundnuts.
Clay soils - Lake shores. Good for rice, vegetables with irrigation.
Fertile volcanic soils - Mt Elgon slopes. Excellent for Arabica coffee.

PESTS & DISEASES:
Maize: stalk borer (use Bulldock), fall armyworm (use Coragen), maize streak virus (plant resistant varieties).
Beans: bean fly, bean mosaic virus. Use certified seed and rotate crops.
Cassava: cassava mosaic disease, cassava brown streak. Plant resistant NARO varieties.

NARO (National Agriculture Research Organisation):
Provides free farming information. Zonal offices in each region.
Website: www.naro.go.ug | Tel: 0414 320 512
Free seeds and seedlings programs periodically available.

=== HEALTH ===

MALARIA:
Most common disease in Uganda. Caused by plasmodium parasite, spread by female Anopheles mosquito.
Symptoms: fever, chills, headache, vomiting, body aches, sweating.
Treatment: Go to health centre immediately. Confirmed with RDT test. Treated with Coartem (free at govt clinics).
Prevention: Sleep under TREATED mosquito net (free from health centres). Drain stagnant water. Use repellent.
NEVER self-medicate with chloroquine alone - malaria is now resistant to it.

TYPHOID:
Spread through contaminated water and food.
Symptoms: High fever (39-40°C), headache, weakness, stomach pain, sometimes rash.
Prevention: Boil drinking water. Wash hands with soap. Avoid street food from unhygienic places.
Treatment: Go to clinic for blood test (Widal test). Treated with ciprofloxacin or azithromycin.

CHOLERA:
Spread through contaminated water. Can kill within hours.
Symptoms: Severe watery diarrhoea (rice-water stool), vomiting, muscle cramps.
Emergency: Go to hospital IMMEDIATELY. Give ORS (oral rehydration salts) on the way.
Prevention: Drink boiled/treated water only. Wash hands. Cook food thoroughly.

HIV/AIDS:
Uganda has made major progress. Treatment (ARVs) is FREE at all government health centres.
Test for free at any health centre - confidential.
Positive? Start ARVs immediately. With treatment, you can live a normal healthy life.
Uganda AIDS Commission: 0800 100 066 (toll free)

MATERNAL HEALTH:
Antenatal care is FREE at all government health centres. Attend at least 4 visits.
1st visit: As soon as you know you are pregnant (before 12 weeks).
Deliver at a health facility - it is safer. Emergency obstetric care is free.
Postnatal care: Visit health centre within 48 hours after delivery, then at 6 weeks.

IMMUNISATION SCHEDULE (free for all children):
Birth: BCG (TB), Polio 0, Hepatitis B
6 weeks: Penta 1, Polio 1, Pneumococcal 1, Rotavirus 1
10 weeks: Penta 2, Polio 2, Pneumococcal 2, Rotavirus 2
14 weeks: Penta 3, Polio 3, Pneumococcal 3
9 months: Measles, Yellow Fever, Vitamin A
All vaccines are FREE at government health centres.

=== LAW & GOVERNMENT ===

UGANDA CONSTITUTION - KEY RIGHTS:
Article 21: All persons are equal before the law.
Article 24: No person shall be subjected to torture or cruel treatment.
Article 26: Every person has the right to own property.
Article 28: Every person has the right to a fair hearing.
Article 29: Every person has freedom of speech, expression, thought, and religion.
Article 33: Women have equal rights to men in all areas.
Article 34: Children have the right to education, protection from abuse.

LAND LAW (Land Act 1998):
Uganda has 4 land tenure systems:
1. Mailo Land - mainly Buganda. Registered to families/individuals. Common in Central Uganda.
2. Freehold - you own land outright with a title deed.
3. Leasehold - you lease land from government or Mailo owner (typically 49 or 99 years).
4. Customary - most of northern and eastern Uganda. Governed by community traditions.

LAND RIGHTS - WOMEN:
Women have equal rights to own land under the Constitution.
A husband CANNOT sell or mortgage family land without wife's written consent (Land Amendment Act 2004).
If your husband dies, you have the right to remain on the matrimonial land.

EVICTION RIGHTS:
A landlord CANNOT evict a tenant without a court order.
If threatened with illegal eviction, report to LC1 Chairman, Police, or Uganda Land Commission.
Uganda Land Commission: 0414 233 435

LOCAL COUNCIL SYSTEM (LC):
LC1 - Village level. Chairman handles small community disputes.
LC2 - Parish level.
LC3 - Sub-county level. Handles land matters, small claims.
LC4 - County level.
LC5 - District level. District Chairman.

For any local dispute, start at LC1 (your village chairman). It is free and accessible.

POLICE & LEGAL AID:
Uganda Police Force emergency: 999 or 0800 199 699 (toll free)
Uganda Law Society legal aid: 0414 259 732
Legal Aid Service Providers Network: 0800 200 888 (toll free)
If arrested: You have the right to remain silent. You have the right to a lawyer.

FAMILY LAW:
Marriage must be consented to by both parties.
Minimum marriage age: 18 years for both men and women.
Bride price (kwanjula) is cultural - not legally required for a valid marriage.
Divorce: File at Family Division of High Court. Grounds include adultery, cruelty, desertion.

VOTER RIGHTS:
Every Ugandan citizen aged 18+ has the right to vote.
Register at your nearest Electoral Commission office with your National ID.
Voting is by secret ballot - nobody can force you to vote for anyone.
Electoral Commission: 0800 199 300 (toll free)

=== GOVERNMENT SERVICES ===

NATIONAL ID (NIRA):
Required documents: Introduction letter from LC1, proof of citizenship (birth certificate or baptism card).
Apply at nearest NIRA office. Processing takes 2-4 weeks.
National ID is FREE for first-time applicants.
NIRA helpline: 0800 100 002 (toll free)
NIRA offices: Kampala (Kololo), all district headquarters.

BIRTH CERTIFICATE:
Register birth within 3 months at nearest NIRA office or health centre.
Required: Hospital delivery note or LC1 introduction letter, parents' IDs.
Cost: Free if registered within 3 months. UGX 20,000 if late registration.

PASSPORT:
Apply online at www.immigration.go.ug or at Directorate of Citizenship in Kampala (Kololo).
Requirements: National ID, two passport photos, application form, payment receipt.
Cost: UGX 250,000 (ordinary, 5 years) | UGX 400,000 (emergency, 3 days)
East African passport: UGX 150,000

URA (UGANDA REVENUE AUTHORITY) - TAX:
Individual Tax Identification Number (TIN): Apply free at www.ura.go.ug or any URA office.
TIN required for: opening business, importing goods, getting government contracts.
If you earn below UGX 235,000 per month, you pay no income tax.
URA helpline: 0800 117 000 (toll free)

UMEME (ELECTRICITY):
New connection: Apply at nearest UMEME office. Provide LC1 letter, national ID, wiring certificate.
Pay bills: MTN Mobile Money Pay Bill - 303303 | Airtel Money - 303303
Report power outage: 0800 200 900 (toll free, 24 hours)

NWSC (NATIONAL WATER & SEWERAGE):

2. Register at URSB: Business name registration costs UGX 30,000-100,000

=== EDUCATION ===

EDUCATION SYSTEM:
Primary school: 7 years (P1-P7). Ends with PLE (Primary Leaving Examinations).
O-Level: 4 years (S1-S4). Ends with UCE (Uganda Certificate of Education).
A-Level: 2 years (S5-S6). Ends with UACE (Uganda Advanced Certificate of Education).
University: 3-5 years depending on course.

UPE (UNIVERSAL PRIMARY EDUCATION):
All government primary schools are free under UPE.
Government pays: tuition, some scholastic materials.
Parents pay: uniforms, lunch, some exercise books.
No child should be sent away from school for not paying UPE fees.

USE (UNIVERSAL SECONDARY EDUCATION):
Free O-Level education in government secondary schools.
Students only pay: lunch money, some materials.

PLE SUBJECTS: English, Mathematics, Science, Social Studies.
For distinction: aim for aggregate 4 (all 1s). Division 1: aggregate 4-12.

UCE SUBJECTS: Must pass: English, Mathematics. Plus at least 6 other subjects.
Division 1: aggregate 7-17 points in 8 subjects.

KEY UNIVERSITIES:
Makerere University - top public university. Admission via PUJAB (0414 531 543).
Kyambogo University - teacher training, technical education.
Mbarara University of Science and Technology (MUST) - medicine, science.
Uganda Martyrs University (UMU) - private, Nkozi.
NKUMBA University - business, social sciences.

SCHOLARSHIPS:
Government scholarship: Top PLE and UCE students. Apply through your school.
Mastercard Foundation Scholars: Makerere University. Full scholarship.
Government of Uganda Higher Education loans: Apply at HELSB (0800 100 005 toll free).
Africa Development Bank scholarships: www.afdb.org/scholarships

=== CULTURE & COMMUNITY ===

LUGANDA PROVERBS (Engero):
"Abantu nibabo" - People are people (Ubuntu - community matters).
"Omuntu w'amagezi abuuza" - A wise person asks questions.
"Ekitalo ky'omuntu omulungi, okumanya" - The glory of a good person is knowledge.
"Obutemu bw'omuntu sibwa sente" - A person's worth is not money.
"Gavumenti etuyamba" - The government helps us (said humorously when it doesn't).

BUGANDA CULTURE:
Kabaka is the cultural leader of Buganda. Current Kabaka: Ronald Muwenda Mutebi II (since 1993).
Buganda Kingdom headquarters: Mengo Palace, Kampala.
Clans (Ebika): Buganda has 52 clans. Each person belongs to their father's clan.
Totem (Omuziro): Each clan has a totem animal they don't eat. e.g., Ngo (Leopard clan), Mbogo (Buffalo clan).
Kwanjula: Traditional introduction ceremony before marriage.
Kika ky'Empindi: Clan gathering for naming ceremonies, last funeral rites.

BANYANKOLE CULTURE (Western Uganda):
Omugabe was the traditional king of Ankole.
Enganzi is the prime minister equivalent.
Famous for long-horned Ankole cattle - a symbol of wealth.
Ekitaguriro is the traditional dance.

ACHOLI CULTURE (Northern Uganda):
Rwot is the traditional chief.
Acholi are known for the Bwola dance - performed at royal ceremonies.
Reconciliation ceremony: Mato Oput - drinking bitter herb to reconcile enemies.

NATIONAL DAYS:
January 26 - NRM Liberation Day (public holiday)
March 8 - International Women's Day
April 11 - Idi Amin Ouster Day (not official holiday)
June 3 - Martyrs Day (public holiday) - celebrated at Namugongo
June 9 - National Heroes Day
October 9 - Independence Day (public holiday) - Uganda independent from Britain 1962

RELIGION:
Uganda is predominantly Christian (~85%) and Muslim (~14%).
Major churches: Catholic, Anglican (COU), Pentecostal (Balokole), Seventh Day Adventist.
Islam: Largest mosque - Gaddafi Mosque (Old Kampala). Uganda Muslim Supreme Council.
Religious harmony is generally good in Uganda.

=== ENVIRONMENT ===

UGANDA'S GEOGRAPHY:
"The Pearl of Africa" - named by Winston Churchill.
Population: approximately 48 million people (2024 estimate).
Capital: Kampala. Largest city by population.
Area: 241,038 sq km. Landlocked but has many lakes.
Lake Victoria: World's second largest freshwater lake. Shared with Kenya and Tanzania.
Mountains: Rwenzori Mountains (snow-capped), Mt Elgon, Virunga volcanoes.
National language: English (official), Luganda (most widely spoken).

RAINY SEASONS:
Central/Southern Uganda: Two rainy seasons - March-May and September-November.
Northern Uganda: One long rainy season - April-October.
Western Uganda: Rain throughout year, heaviest March-May.
Rainfall warning: Climate change is making seasons less predictable. Plant early.

NATIONAL PARKS & WILDLIFE:
Bwindi Impenetrable Forest: Mountain gorillas. UNESCO World Heritage Site.
Queen Elizabeth National Park: Elephants, lions, hippos, tree-climbing lions.
Murchison Falls National Park: Largest park. Nile River, hippos, crocodiles, Shoebill stork.
Kidepo Valley National Park: Most remote. Cheetahs, lions, giraffes.
Uganda Wildlife Authority: 0414 355 000

ENVIRONMENTAL ISSUES:
Deforestation: Uganda has lost 60% of forest cover. Plant trees - it is law.
Wetland destruction: Illegal to build on wetlands. Report to NEMA: 0800 991 001.
Plastic bags: Single-use plastic bags are BANNED in Uganda since 2009.
Water hyacinth: Invasive plant choking Lake Victoria. Report to National Fisheries Resources Research Institute.
